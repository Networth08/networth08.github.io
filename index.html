Tua Tagovailoa

 

Early Childhood

     Tua Tagovailoa was born on March 2, 1998, in Ewa Beach, Hawaii. He is the oldest of four children in his athletic family. Tua grew up loving the game of football, as he would sleep with a football under his blanket every night. His father, Galu, trained him at the age of two, forcing him to throw with his left hand instead of his right. His biggest inspiration growing up as a kid was his grandfather, Seu, also known as "Chief Tagovailoa".

     The first year of Varsity football for Tua, he threw 33 touchdowns with only 3 interceptions, and a shocking 2,583 passisng yards. His motivation was from his father's dicipline, as he would use a belt on him everytime Tua threw an interception. He set many records and received many awards while playing High School football in Hawaii.

College Career

     In 2017, Tua Tagovailoa was the backup quarter back at the University of Alabama. In the meantime, quarterback Jalen Hurts was in the starting position for Alabama. Tua had little playing time only through some blowout victories for the Tide. On September 9, 2017, Tagovailoa made his College debut against Fresno State. Throughout the regular football season at Alabama, Tagovailoa showed out at the quarterback position as a backup. On January 8, 2018, Alabama was playing in the College Football Playoff National Championship in Atlanta, Geargia. In the meantime, Tua was still the backup, and the Tide was facing off against the Bulldogs of Georgia. After the first half of the National Championship, Geargia was leading Alabama 13-0. During halftime, Coach Nick Saban, made the decision to change quarterbacks and start Tagovailoa for the second half. Tagovailoa came out hot and led the Tide into overtime in the National Chamionship. In overtime, Tua coverted a long 2nd & 26, and threw the game winning touchdown to give the Tide a 26-23 victory of the Bulldogs.

     In 2018, Tua Tagovailoa was named the starting quarterback at the start of the season. Throughout the season, Tagovailoa lived up to his standards, and had a record breaking year. Although some injuries throughout the season, Tua still dominated the playing field. He suffered an ankle injury in the SEC Championship game that set him back a couple of weeks. The Tide won the SEC championship and then went on to the National Championship later that year. Unfortunately, the Tide could not get things done, and ended up losing to the Clemson Tigers. Tua finished the year finishing 2nd in the Heisman race, behind quarterback Kyler Murray.

     In 2019, Tua Tagovailoa was the same quarterback we saw before his injury in the previous year. The stats, in 2019, were off the roof, getting the attention of NFL scouts. Throughout the season, there were many projections of Tua going number 1 in the Upcoming NFL draft that year. Although another injury against Tennessee, setting him back for a couple of games. Tua returned the next couple of weeks, and still dominated the opposing team´s defenses. While facing off against Mississippi State that year, Tua had a devasting injury. He scrambled outside of the pocket when finally two defenders tackled him. When Tua went down, He suffered a dislocated hip, a broken nose, and a concussion. Tagovailoa was on pace to have a Historical year not only for Alabama, but for the entire College Football world.
